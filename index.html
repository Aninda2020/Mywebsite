Research Experience

Modulation of bacterial rhomboid protease function by membrane lipids using co-evolutionary mechanisms

PhD Candidate, University of Virginia (Advisor: Prof. Linda Columbus) | 2023 – Present

Currently investigating how membrane lipids influence the function of bacterial rhomboid proteases. This research employs co-evolutionary analysis and various biophysical techniques to understand these complex interactions at a molecular level.

Biophysical Chemistry Membrane Proteins Rhomboid Protease Lipid Interaction Co-evolution

Cryo-EM studies of Shigella Phage Sfk20

Junior Research Fellow, ICMR-NIRBI, Kolkata, India (Guide: Dr. Moumita Dutta) | Sep 2021 – Mar 2023

Standardized sample preparation protocols for Cryo-Electron Microscopy (Cryo-EM) of a newly isolated Shigella Phage Sfk20. Investigated the stability and host interaction of bacteriophages under cryo-conditions using Cryo-EM imaging. Additionally, established a computational pipeline for cryo-electron tomography data processing.

Cryo-EM Bacteriophage Shigella Structural Biology Electron Tomography

Role of Elongation Factor-G1 in Mycobacterial Translation

Dissertation Student, CSIR-IICB, Kolkata, India (Guide: Dr. Jayati Sengupta) | Jan 2020 – June 2021

Successfully purified Msm Elongation factor-G1 (EF-G1), which promotes the translocation step in bacterial protein synthesis. Investigated the role of EF-G1 in bacterial protein synthesis during stress conditions and conducted biochemical studies to observe its binding to the Msm 70S ribosome.

Protein Synthesis Elongation Factor Mycobacteria Ribosome Biochemistry
